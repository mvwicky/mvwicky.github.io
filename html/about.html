This is a blog that I started as a place to record some thoughts, ideas, stories, baseball musings, and basically any other things that come to mind that I feel compelled to share.

Baseball, it is one of my most intense passions, which is why it has a section dedicated to it. I grew up a Los Angeles Dodgers fan, and as I have gotten older my love of both the Dodgers and the game itself has grown exponentially. With this expansion of my, now, obsession with the game, I have become engrossed with statistics and Sabermetric analysis of players. The articles that I post about baseball will be on all topics of the game from its most emotional aspects to its most cold number crunching side.

The stories section is dedicated to any narrative content that I happen to come up with and feel is worthy of sharing. I have a contentious relationship with writing as I love to do it but am often overcome with feelings of inadequacy and writer's block. When I do post, the formats will be varied. From treatments, to full on screenplays, to short stories, to the first (or any middle) chapter of a potential novel.

Since this is an about page, I should probably write something about myself. I am twenty years old and a sophomore at Northeastern University. My major is Computer Engineering and I am an avid programmer, writer, and water polo player. Born in Santa Barbara, California, I grew a quarter mile from the beach and my life has been heavily shaped by my relationship with water.
